Hypothesis

We are attempting to catch moths and gather data about different moths with different traps.

How do the moth traps work?

  1. The moths are attracted to the light/UV light

  2. The moths fly towards the light and once they hit it they faint because they do not expect to hit what they thought to be 'the Moon'

  3. The moths fall down onto the angled sheets of plastic and they fall through the gap into the body of the trap

  4. The moths try and fail to find a way out so they rest in the egg cartons

Process

  1. We built some moth traps with perspex/corrugated plastic sheets and the lights to attract the moths

  2. We put cut-up egg cartons in the traps for the moths to sit on

  3. We left the traps on overnight and photographed and documented any moths we found

Basic findings

  • We were not as succsessful on colder nights below 7-8°
  • Our best findings were with the moth trap Daneel's grandfather made

End Result

  • We only caught moths on warmer nights
  • The moths preferred the UV lights
  • We caught a wide variety of moths - usually only brown moths but Daneel ended up catching a green moth that flew away very quckly.
  • Many of the moths flew away quickly after the trap was opened in the morning
